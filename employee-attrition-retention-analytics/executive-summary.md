# Executive Summary — Employee Attrition & Retention Analytics
*Aditi Mishra | MBA Candidate, IIIT Lucknow | People Analytics Project*

---

## The Problem

Employee attrition is one of the most expensive and preventable business problems. Yet most organizations only realize an employee is leaving when they submit their resignation — too late to intervene.

**The cost is massive** *(Assumption — industry benchmark)*:
- Replacing one mid-level employee costs **50–200% of their annual salary**
- Average voluntary attrition rate in Indian IT/services sector: **18–22% annually**
- For a 1,000-person company at ₹8L avg salary: ₹20% attrition = 200 exits = **₹16–32 Cr replacement cost/year**

## What I Built

A **People Analytics Dashboard** analyzing 1,400+ employee records to:
1. Identify the top drivers of voluntary attrition
2. Quantify the cost of turnover by segment
3. Recommend targeted retention interventions

## Key Findings *(Dataset: IBM HR Analytics — publicly available)*

| Finding | Insight |
|---|---|
| Top attrition driver | Overtime (employees working overtime: 30% attrition vs. 10% non-overtime) |
| Highest-risk role | Sales Representatives (39% attrition rate) |
| Tenure risk window | Employees with 0–2 years tenure: 33% attrition |
| Compensation signal | Below-median salary band: 2.1x higher attrition risk |
| Work-life balance | Score 1 (worst): 31% attrition; Score 4 (best): 17% attrition |

## Strategic Impact

Targeted interventions in top 3 risk segments projected to reduce overall attrition by **8–12 percentage points** — saving ₹6–10 Cr annually for a mid-size organization *(assumption-labeled)*.
