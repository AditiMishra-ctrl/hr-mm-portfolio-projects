## 60-Second Pitch

"Recruitment efficiency is a talent strategy problem, not just an HR ops problem. I analyzed a full hiring funnel — from 1,000 applications to 18 hires — and found three critical failures.

First, time-to-hire was 34 days vs. a 15-day industry benchmark. Top candidates accept offers within 10–14 days, so we were losing the best people during our own process.

Second, the Interview-to-Offer conversion was only 29.5% — well below the 40–50% benchmark. Root cause: no structured evaluation rubric, leading to inconsistent interviewer decisions and a 64% inter-rater reliability rate.

Third, employee referrals — our highest quality-of-hire channel at 4.3 out of 5 rating and zero cost — represented only 12% of applications and 33% of hires.

My solutions: a competency-based scorecard targeting 85% inter-rater reliability, SLA framework to cut time-to-hire to under 15 days, and a referral incentive program that reduces cost-per-hire from ₹14,400 to ₹9,000."

## STAR Story

**Situation**: I analyzed a recruitment process that was struggling with slow hiring timelines, inconsistent candidate evaluation, and high cost-per-hire — common pain points in talent acquisition that directly impact business performance.

**Task**: Map the end-to-end recruitment funnel, identify the key failure points, and design structural improvements to hiring quality, speed, and cost-efficiency.

**Action**:
- Mapped 5-stage funnel; calculated conversion rates and time-per-stage; benchmarked against industry standards
- Identified 3 root causes: 34-day time-to-hire (2.3x benchmark), 29.5% Interview→Offer conversion (below 40% benchmark), referral underutilization (only 12% of applications)
- Analyzed sourcing channel quality: referrals = 4.3/5 quality, ₹0 cost; LinkedIn = 3.8/5, ₹18,000/hire
- Designed competency-based scorecard (5 dimensions, weighted, behavioral anchors) to improve inter-rater reliability from 64% to 85%
- Built structured 5-question phone screening rubric with standardized pass criteria
- Recommended referral program incentive (₹10,000/hire) — still 44% cheaper than LinkedIn

**Result**: A complete recruitment process redesign targeting: time-to-hire <15 days, Interview→Offer conversion from 29.5% to 45%, cost-per-hire from ₹14,400 to ₹9,000, and referral share from 33% to 55% of hires.

## HR Interview Q&A

**Q: How would you measure quality-of-hire?**

"Quality-of-hire is a composite metric I'd define as: (Performance Rating at 90 days + Manager Satisfaction Score + Retention at 12 months) ÷ 3. Each on a 1–5 scale. This gives a single number per hire that's trackable by sourcing channel and interviewer. If referrals consistently score 4.3 and job portals score 3.4, that informs sourcing budget allocation."

**Q: How do you reduce bias in hiring without removing human judgment?**

"Structure, not elimination. The competency scorecard doesn't remove human judgment — it channels it into consistent, pre-defined criteria with behavioral anchors. Before the scorecard, interviewers were evaluating different things. After it, they're evaluating the same 5 competencies against the same evidence standard. I'd also recommend panel calibration sessions — where 3 interviewers independently score the same candidate and then discuss gaps. That surfaces bias patterns without making anyone feel accused."

**Q: Offer decline rate is 23%. What do you do?**

"First, understand why — a 3-question post-decline anonymous survey: Was it compensation? Role clarity? A competing offer? Timeline? That data shapes the intervention. If it's compensation, we need a market benchmarking review. If it's role clarity, we fix the JD and improve pre-offer conversations. If it's competing offers, we need to cut time-to-hire. The worst thing is assuming you know why candidates decline without asking them."
