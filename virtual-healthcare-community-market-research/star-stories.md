The gap is not technology or price — it's trust. 68% said they don't trust online diagnosis, and 54% don't know if the doctor is actually qualified.

## Strategy / BD Interview Q&A

**Q: The trust gap is huge. How do you actually build trust for a new healthcare platform?**

"Three mechanisms. First, verified credentials — every doctor's MBBS/MD registration publicly visible, not just 'verified by us.' Second, community proof — peer reviews from real patients with specific experience details, not star ratings. Third, continuity — users see the same doctor every time, not a random rotating roster. Trust in healthcare is built through repetition and accountability. A platform that embeds all three creates a defensible moat that discovery-only platforms like Practo can't easily replicate."

**Q: How would you handle the regulatory risk from the Telemedicine Practice Guidelines?**

"Turn it into a feature. The 2020 guidelines restrict first prescriptions without prior in-person consultation — that's actually a trust signal we can lean into. 'We follow NMC guidelines — your health is safe here.' The DPDP Act compliance is table stakes. I'd appoint a DPO, publish a plain-language privacy policy, and give users granular consent controls. Regulatory alignment in healthcare isn't a burden — it's marketing."

**Q: Market is growing at 27% CAGR. Would you wait for it to mature or enter now?**

"Enter now, for two reasons. First, the trust infrastructure we build — community, doctor relationships, brand — takes 2–3 years to compound. Starting now means we're entrenched when the market matures. Second, the government tailwind (ABDM, NDHM) is providing infrastructure that accelerates the category. Early entrants who align with ABDM get distribution advantages (ABHA-linked health records) that latecomers can't easily replicate."
