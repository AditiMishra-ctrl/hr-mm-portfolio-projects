# Case Study — Employee Engagement & DEI Survey Study & Action Plan
*Aditi Mishra | Survey Design, People Analytics, Google Forms*

---

## 1. Problem Statement

**How can an organization measure the true state of employee engagement and DEI — and translate those findings into targeted, actionable improvements?**

Employee engagement surveys often fail in two ways: they ask the wrong questions (generic), or they produce a report that gets filed and forgotten. This project was designed to avoid both failures — with a structured survey methodology and a prioritized action plan tied directly to findings.

---

## 2. Survey Design

### Methodology
- **Tool**: Google Forms
- **Respondents**: 50+ employees across departments and seniority levels
- **Distribution**: Shared via department heads; guaranteed anonymity
- **Duration**: 7 days open

### Survey Sections

#### Section 1: Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS)
> "On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend this organization as a great place to work?"
- Promoters (9–10), Passives (7–8), Detractors (0–6)
- **eNPS = % Promoters − % Detractors**

#### Section 2: Core Engagement Drivers (5-point Likert scale)
1. I understand how my work contributes to company goals
2. I have the resources I need to do my job effectively
3. My manager gives me regular, useful feedback
4. I have opportunities to grow and develop here
5. I feel recognized for good work
6. My workload is manageable
7. I would recommend this organization to a friend *(eNPS backing question)*

#### Section 3: Manager Effectiveness
1. My manager sets clear expectations
2. My manager supports my professional development
3. My manager treats team members fairly and consistently
4. I feel comfortable sharing concerns with my manager

#### Section 4: DEI Section
1. I feel respected regardless of my background, gender, or identity
2. I believe promotions and opportunities are distributed fairly
3. I feel comfortable being myself at work
4. My team values diverse perspectives
5. Open-ended: "What would make this a more inclusive workplace?"

#### Section 5: Work-Life Balance
1. I can maintain a healthy boundary between work and personal life
2. I rarely feel burned out at work
3. I have flexibility when I need it for personal matters

---

## 3. Key Findings *(50 respondents)*

### eNPS Result
- Promoters (9–10): 28% → 14 respondents
- Passives (7–8): 44% → 22 respondents
- Detractors (0–6): 28% → 14 respondents
- **eNPS = 28% − 28% = 0** *(Industry benchmark for good engagement: +20 to +40)*

**Interpretation**: An eNPS of 0 means the organization is not creating enough promoters. It's not a crisis, but it's a clear signal that the engagement experience needs intentional improvement.

---

### Top Engagement Drivers — Scores

| Engagement Item | Avg Score (1–5) | Status |
|---|---|---|
| I understand how my work contributes to goals | 3.8 | ✅ Strength |
| I have resources I need | 3.2 | ⚠️ Needs attention |
| Manager gives regular feedback | 2.9 | 🔴 Low |
| Opportunities to grow | 2.7 | 🔴 Low — Priority |
| I feel recognized for good work | 2.6 | 🔴 Low — Priority |
| Workload is manageable | 2.8 | 🔴 Low |

**Key Finding**: Recognition and Growth opportunity are the two lowest-scoring items — and both are actionable with minimal budget.

---

### Manager Effectiveness Scores

| Item | Score |
|---|---|
| Manager sets clear expectations | 3.4 |
| Supports professional development | 2.8 |
| Treats team fairly | 3.5 |
| I can share concerns with manager | 3.0 |

**Finding**: Manager development support is a gap. Employees don't feel their managers actively invest in their growth. This directly ties to the "Growth Opportunities" low score above.

---

### DEI Findings

| Item | Score | Flag |
|---|---|---|
| I feel respected regardless of background | 3.9 | ✅ |
| Promotions are distributed fairly | 2.8 | ⚠️ Perception gap |
| I feel comfortable being myself | 3.5 | ✅ |
| Team values diverse perspectives | 3.6 | ✅ |

**Open-ended common themes** (from 34 written responses):
- "More diverse leadership representation needed" (mentioned by 12 respondents)
- "Mentorship for women in technical roles" (mentioned by 8)
- "Clearer promotion criteria" (mentioned by 15 respondents)

---

### At-Risk Groups (Segmentation)

| Segment | eNPS | Engagement Avg |
|---|---|---|
| Employees, 0–1 year tenure | -12 | 2.6/5 |
| Individual Contributors (non-managers) | -8 | 2.8/5 |
| Female employees | -5 | 3.0/5 |
| Senior employees (5+ years) | +22 | 3.8/5 |

**Finding**: New employees and individual contributors are the most disengaged — early experience and career progression are the key levers.

## 4. Prioritized Action Plan

### Priority 1: Recognition Program (High Impact, Low Cost)
- **Problem**: Recognition score = 2.6/5 (lowest of all items)
- **Action**: Launch a peer recognition program ("Kudos Board" — digital or physical); manager training on specific, timely recognition; monthly "impact highlight" in all-hands
- **Target**: Recognition score from 2.6 → 3.5 in 6 months

### Priority 2: Transparent Growth & Promotion Pathways
- **Problem**: Growth score 2.7/5; promotion fairness score 2.8/5; "unclear promotion criteria" mentioned by 15 respondents
- **Action**: Publish role-level competency frameworks; quarterly career conversations mandatory for all managers; internal mobility bulletin
- **Target**: Growth score from 2.7 → 3.4; promotion fairness from 2.8 → 3.5

### Priority 3: Manager Development Program
- **Problem**: Manager development support score 2.8/5; directly linked to both growth and engagement gaps
- **Action**: Manager training on giving developmental feedback; quarterly skip-level meetings; manager effectiveness score tracked in performance review
- **Target**: Manager development support score 2.8 → 3.6

### Priority 4: DEI — Clearer Promotion Criteria
- **Problem**: 15 respondents mentioned unclear promotion criteria as a DEI issue (perception of favoritism)
- **Action**: Publish objective promotion rubric; diversity target in promotion slate (min 40% women in leadership pipeline); DEI dashboard visible to all employees quarterly
- **Target**: Promotion fairness score 2.8 → 3.5; women in leadership +8pp

---

## 5. Measurement Plan

- **Pulse Survey**: 5-question pulse survey every 90 days to track progress on priority items
- **eNPS Quarterly**: Track eNPS trend; target +20 in 12 months
- **Action Accountability**: Each action has an owner, deadline, and success metric
- **Annual Full Survey**: Repeat full survey annually to track year-over-year trends
